# Comparative Analysis of Antioxidant Capacity and Melanogenesis-Inhibitory Efficacy

This repository contains the raw experimental data and statistical analysis results for the research paper: **"Comparative Analysis of Antioxidant Capacity and Melanogenesis-Inhibitory Efficacy of Five Medicinal Plant Extracts"**, published in the *Journal of Enlightenment Science & Technology (JEST)*.

## 📂 Dataset Overview

The dataset includes results from antioxidant assays (DPPH, ABTS), cell viability tests (MTT assay), and melanogenesis inhibition assays (Melanin content) conducted on *B16F10 murine melanoma cells* and cell-free systems.

### 🌿 Plant Extracts Abbreviations
The following abbreviations are used throughout the data files:
* **RT-E**: *Rhodomyrtus tomentosa* 
* **RP-E**: *Rubus parvifolius* 
* **CR-E**: *Rosa laevigata* (Cherokee Rose)
* **IA-E**: *Ilex asprella* 
* **FE-E**: *Eriobotrya japonica*

## 📁 File Description

The data files are categorized by experimental assay:

### 1. Antioxidant Capacity Assays
Data related to the free radical scavenging abilities of the extracts.

* **`ABTS.xlsx`**: Raw data of ABTS radical scavenging activity (% inhibition) at various concentrations.
* **`ABTS IC50.xlsx`**: Calculated Half-maximal Inhibitory Concentration ($IC_{50}$) values for the ABTS assay.
* **`DPPH.xlsx`**: Raw data of DPPH radical scavenging activity (% inhibition) for individual extracts.
* **`DPPH IC50.xlsx`**: Calculated $IC_{50}$ values for the DPPH assay.
* **`DPPH combine.xlsx`**: Data for combinatorial antioxidant tests (e.g., CRE combined with RPE or RTE) to investigate synergistic effects.

### 2. Cell Viability / Cytotoxicity (MTT Assay)
Data assessing the safety and non-toxic concentrations of the extracts on B16F10 cells.

* **`MTT pretest.xlsx`**: Preliminary broad-range cytotoxicity screening (0–200 µg/mL) to determine safe working concentrations.
* **`MTT.xlsx`**: Detailed cell viability percentages for individual extracts at refined concentration ranges.
* **`MTT combine.xlsx`**: Cell viability data for the combinatorial treatments (e.g., CRE + RPE) to ensure safety in combined applications.

### 3. Melanogenesis Inhibition
Data evaluating the efficacy of extracts in reducing melanin synthesis.

* **`Melanin assay.xlsx`**: Intracellular melanin content measurements in $\alpha$-MSH-stimulated B16F10 cells treated with various concentrations of extracts.

---

## 🧪 Experimental Notes

* **Cell Line**: B16F10 murine melanoma cells.
* **Stimulation**: Cells were stimulated with $\alpha$-MSH (alpha-Melanocyte-stimulating hormone) to induce melanogenesis.
* **Positive Controls**: Ascorbic Acid (Vitamin C) was used as a positive control for antioxidant assays.
* **Statistical Analysis**: All experiments were performed in at least triplicate. Data are presented as Mean ± SD.
